Early History

The British Isles were originally inhabited by Celtic tribes. In 43 AD, the Romans invaded Britain and ruled much of it for almost 400 years. After the Romans left, Anglo-Saxon tribes settled in England.

Norman Conquest (1066)

In 1066, William the Conqueror from Normandy invaded England. This event changed England’s language, culture, and political system, and strengthened the monarchy.

  • 1707: England and Scotland officially united to form Great Britain.

  • 1801: Great Britain united with Ireland, creating the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland.

British Empire

From the 17th to the early 20th century, Britain built the largest empire in history, ruling territories across Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Oceania. This made the UK a major global power.
Ireland and Modern UK

  • 1921: Most of Ireland became independent.

  • The UK became the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, which is its current form.

20th Century

The UK played a major role in World War I and World War II. After WWII, the empire declined, and many colonies became independent.
Recent History

  • The UK joined the European Union in 1973.

  • In 2016, the UK voted to leave the EU.

  • The UK officially left the EU in 2020 (Brexit).

Today

Today, the UK is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary democracy. It remains influential in politics, culture, finance, and education worldwide.
