The original Westminster Palace burned down in 1834. The current building rises from its ashes, and it took 48 years to rebuild it, taking us to the year 1888. This palace is in the Victorian Gothic style and contains 1,200 rooms, 11 courtyards, and 3.5 kilometers of corridors; currently, this is where the two Houses of the British Parliament are located. On one side of the Palace is the Big Ben tower, which was built in 1858 and stands 106 meters tall. It is one of the most iconic buildings in London and has 4 clocks, one on each side of the tower.

Additionally, as a curiosity, its name comes from the fact that its clocks can be seen from anywhere in London from the tower of St. Stephen.
